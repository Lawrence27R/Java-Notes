**Agile Model**



Agile is a software development methodology where the project is developed in small iterations (Sprints) and feedback is taken continuously from stakeholders.2-4 weeks

**Requirement Gathering**

Product Owner gathers requirements from the client.

Requirements are written as User Stories.

EG - As a user, I want to reset my password so that I can regain access to my account.

**Product Backlog Creation**

All requirements are added to a Product Backlog.

Product Backlog is a prioritized list of features, bugs, and enhancements.

**Sprint Planning**

Team selects high-priority items from the Product Backlog.

Sprint duration is usually 1–4 weeks.

**Output:**

Sprint Goal

**Development**

Developers start implementing the selected user stories.

**Activities:**

Coding

Unit Testing

Code Review

**Daily Standup Meeting**

A 15-minute daily meeting where each team member answers:

What did I do yesterday?

What will I do today?

Any blockers?

**Testing**

QA/Testers verify:

Functionality

Bugs

Performance

Regression Testing

Any defects are added back to the backlog.

**Sprint Review / Demo**

Team demonstrates completed features to stakeholders.

Feedback is collected.

Example:

Show the newly developed Password Reset functionality to the client.

**Sprint Retrospective**

Team discusses:

What went well?

What didn't go well?

What can be improved?

**Release / Deployment**

Once features are approved:

Deploy to QA/UAT

Deploy to Production

**Repeat the Cycle**



**Product Owner (PO)**

Defines requirements.

Prioritizes backlog.

Represents business needs.



**Scrum Master**

Facilitates Agile process.

Removes blockers.

Conducts Scrum ceremonies.



**Development Team**

Developers

Testers

DevOps Engineers



**What we followed**

We don't strictly follow Agile or Scrum. However, our team has a daily status meeting with the manager, team lead, architect, developers, and testers. During the meeting, each team member shares what they worked on the previous day, what they plan to work on today, and any issues or dependencies they are facing. This helps the team stay aligned, track progress, and resolve blockers quickly.
